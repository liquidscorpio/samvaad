Castastrophic Healthcare Expenditure

Analysis into vulnerability of the general populace

A cursory look into the dataset listed by The World Bank tells a rather surprising story: People in developed regions like Nothern America and continental Europe are exposed higher healthcare expenses from household pockets as against Africa and Asia. It tells only a part of the story and the reality is much more depressing.

When adjusted for Per Capita Income as published by The World Bank itself, the choropleth is almost entirely inverted. The reason lies in absymally low incomes in many parts of Africa and Asia leading to miniscule budgets and consequently low out-of-pocket expenses on healthcare.
